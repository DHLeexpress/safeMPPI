\begin{abstract}
Sampling-based predictive control (SBPC), such as model predictive path integral (MPPI) control, is efficient but cannot enforce hard safety constraints: individual rollouts may be safe while their reward-weighted average is not. We build a pipeline that (i) turns MPPI into a provably safe, safety-\emph{tunable} data engine, (ii) distills it into a real-time generative policy, and (iii) \emph{certifiably expands} that policy. First, our MPPI-DCBF planner rejects rollouts that violate a discrete-time control barrier function (DCBF) built from a robot-centered \emph{free-space polytope}, producing a rich dataset of multi-modal, collision-free behaviors modulated by a single safety parameter $\gamma$. A conditional flow matching (CFM) model then learns a policy that is tunable by $\gamma$ at inference without online sampling. To move beyond statistical safety, we introduce a \emph{compact polytope certificate}: a small second-order cone program (SOCP) that fits a maximum-margin safe polytope tube around a generated trajectory, so that \keyword{the existence of a certified sample proves the plan is safe}. Finally, \keyword{Safe Flow Expansion} uses this certificate as the verifier in a continued-pretraining loop that expands the policy's certified generable set toward new, previously unseen safe modes. We demonstrate the framework on narrow-gap threading and a slalom, where expansion discovers certified go-around and weaving modes absent from the pretraining data.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\IEEEPARstart{S}{ampling-based} predictive control (SBPC) circumvents the curse of dimensionality of gradient-based optimal control by forward-sampling stochastic dynamics and forming a Monte-Carlo, reward-weighted average of the sampled trajectories \cite{williams2017model, howell2022predictive}. MPPI \cite{williams2016aggressive} in particular scales to dexterous manipulation and legged locomotion on parallel hardware \cite{li2025drop, xue2025full, kurtz2024hydrax}. Despite this, MPPI cannot natively enforce hard safety constraints such as collision avoidance: safety is usually encoded as a soft penalty \cite{williams2017model, mohamed2023gp}, but because the executed control is an \emph{average} over samples, individual safe rollouts do not guarantee a safe mean.

A complementary trend distills such planners into generative policies. Diffusion and flow matching synthesize multi-modal robot behavior from demonstrations \cite{kurtz2025generative, chi2023diffusion}, and generative predictive control (GPC) learns a velocity field from noise to control conditioned on observations, applied in a receding-horizon fashion \cite{kurtz2025generative}. Yet these policies inherit safety only statistically from their data and provide no per-rollout guarantee, nor a principled way to trade off safety against feasibility online \cite{dai2025safefm, carvalho2023mpdiffusion}.

This paper closes the loop from a safe planner to a \emph{certifiably safe, expandable} generative policy. Our contributions are: \textbf{(1)} a safety-tunable MPPI-DCBF data engine whose rejection barrier is a robot-centered \emph{free-space polytope} over \emph{all} nearby obstacles, generalizing a single-obstacle affine barrier; \textbf{(2)} a compact-polytope SOCP \emph{certificate} that verifies a generated trajectory post-hoc --- so that a single certified sample proves safety; and \textbf{(3)} \keyword{Safe Flow Expansion}, an active continued-pretraining loop that uses this certificate as its verifier to grow the policy's certified generable set toward new safe modes (Fig.~\ref{fig:keystone}).

\section{Preliminaries}
\subsection{Model Predictive Path Integral Control}
At state $x(0)\in\mathbb{R}^{n_x}$ and step $k$, MPPI approximately solves, over the mean control sequence $\{u_{k+i\mid k}\}\in\mathbb{R}^{n_u}$, $i\in\{0,\dots,N-1\}$,
\begin{subequations}
\label{eq:sc-mppi}
\begin{align}
\min_{\{u_{k+i\mid k}\}}
& \mathbb{E}\!\left[
\phi\!\left(x_{k+N\mid k}\right)
\!+\!\!\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}\!\Big(q(x_{k+i\mid k})\!+\!\tfrac{1}{2}u_{k+i\mid k}^\top R\,u_{k+i\mid k}\Big)
\right]\!, \\
\text{s.t.}\;\;
& x_{k+i+1\mid k}=f\!\left(x_{k+i\mid k},\,u_{k+i\mid k}+\varepsilon_{k+i}\right), \\
& x_{k\mid k}=x(0),\quad \varepsilon_{k+i}\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\,\Sigma).
\end{align}
\end{subequations}
Here $q,\phi$ are running/terminal costs and $R$ a control weight. A warm-start mean $\bar u_{k+i\mid k}$ is perturbed, $u^{(m)}_{k+i\mid k}=\bar u_{k+i\mid k}+\varepsilon^{(m)}_{k+i}$, each rollout $m$ is scored by its cost $S^{(m)}$, and the mean is updated by the information-theoretic weights \cite{williams2017info}
\begin{equation}
\omega_m=\exp\!\Big(\!-\tfrac{1}{\lambda}[S^{(m)}\!-\!\beta]\Big),\;
\Delta u_{k+i}=\frac{\sum_m \omega_m\varepsilon^{(m)}_{k+i}}{\sum_m \omega_m},
\label{eq:mppi-update}
\end{equation}
with $\beta=\min_m S^{(m)}$. The current control $u^*_{k\mid k}$ is applied and the sequence is shifted to warm-start the next step. \keyword{The averaging in \eqref{eq:mppi-update} is the source of unsafety}: even if each rollout avoids obstacles, the weighted mean need not.

\Remark[~1 (sampling matters)]{We draw perturbations from a Gaussian $\varepsilon\sim\mathcal N(0,\Sigma)$ around a warm-started mean $\mu=\bar u$, and \emph{steer $(\mu,\Sigma)$ using the free-space polytope of Sec.~\ref{sec:planner}} --- the mean is biased toward the polytope centroid and the covariance is scaled by the polytope size. We deliberately keep this as ordinary sampling from $\mathcal N(\mu,\Sigma)$ rather than a formal importance-sampling scheme; the diversity of the samples, not a reweighting identity, is what lets $\gamma$ shape multi-modal behavior.}

\subsection{Discrete-Time Control Barrier Functions}
For a safe set $\mathcal C=\{x:h(x)\ge0\}$ and control-affine dynamics $x_{k+1}=f(x_k)+g(x_k)u_k$, $h$ is a discrete-time CBF (DCBF) if there is $\gamma\in(0,1]$ such that, for all $x_k$, some $u_k$ gives
\begin{equation}
h(x_{k+1})-h(x_k)\ge -\gamma\, h(x_k)\;\Longrightarrow\; h(x_{k+i})\ge (1-\gamma)^i h(x_k),
\label{eq:dtcbf}
\end{equation}
so the barrier decays no faster than $(1-\gamma)^i$, keeping $\mathcal C$ forward-invariant \cite{ames2014cbf, agrawal2017discrete}. The parameter $\gamma$ trades safety (small $\gamma$, conservative) against feasibility (large $\gamma$).

\subsection{Conditional Flow Matching}
Flow matching learns a marginal velocity field $u^*_t(x)$ transporting a simple prior $x_0\sim\mathcal N(0,I)$ to a data distribution $z\sim p_{\text{data}}$ by regressing a conditional field along the optimal-transport path $x_t=tz+(1-t)\epsilon$ \cite{lipman2022flow, lipman2024flow}. Conditioning on an observation/context tuple, the conditional flow matching (CFM) loss is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L(\theta)=
\mathbb{E}_{t,\epsilon,(z,o,\gamma,c)}
\Big[\big\|u_t^\theta(tz{+}(1{-}t)\epsilon\mid o,\gamma,c)-(z-\epsilon)\big\|^2\Big],
\label{eq:cfm}
\end{equation}
with target control $z=u^*_{k\mid k}$, observation $o$, safety level $\gamma$, and a sequential context $c$. Sampling integrates the learned ODE from noise, yielding a policy without online rollout sampling.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{Safe MPPI Data Engine}\label{sec:planner}
We first turn MPPI into a safe, $\gamma$-tunable generator (Stage 2). For a convex obstacle, let $p$ be its boundary point nearest $x_{k\mid k}$ with outward normal $n$; the affine DCBF
\begin{equation}
h^{\mathrm{aff}}(x_{k+i\mid k};x_{k\mid k})=\frac{n^\top(x_{k+i\mid k}-p)}{n^\top(x_{k\mid k}-p)}
\label{eq:aff_barrier}
\end{equation}
defines the convex half-space $\mathcal C_i^{\mathrm{aff}}=\{x:h^{\mathrm{aff}}\ge0\}$. Applying \eqref{eq:dtcbf} with $h^{\mathrm{aff}}(x_{k\mid k};x_{k\mid k})=1$ yields the per-rollout safety specification
\begin{equation}
h^{\mathrm{aff}}(x_{k+i\mid k};x_{k\mid k})\ge (1-\gamma)^i.
\label{eq:safetyspec}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Polytope DCBF over all nearby obstacles}
A single-nearest affine barrier re-selects the closest obstacle each step, which is non-smooth in cluttered scenes. We instead build, once at $x_{k\mid k}$, a \keyword{robot-centered free-space polytope} $\mathcal P=\{x:\,a_\ell^\top(x-c)\le b_\ell,\ \ell=1,\dots,K\}$, $c=x_{k\mid k}$, whose $K$ faces are tangent hyperplanes to \emph{all} nearby obstacles (Sec.~\ref{sec:cert} details the geometry). Its smooth barrier is
\begin{equation}
H_{\mathcal P}(x)=\min_{\ell=1,\dots,K}\frac{b_\ell-a_\ell^\top(x-c)}{b_\ell},\qquad H_{\mathcal P}(c)=1,
\label{eq:HP}
\end{equation}
and the rejection rule replaces \eqref{eq:safetyspec} by $H_{\mathcal P}(x^{(m)}_{k+i+1\mid k})\ge(1-\gamma)H_{\mathcal P}(x^{(m)}_{k+i\mid k})$. Algorithm~\ref{alg:safe-mppi} rejects any violating rollout by setting $S^{(m)}\leftarrow\infty$, so that \eqref{eq:mppi-update} averages \emph{only} certified-safe samples.

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{Safe MPPI (MPPI-DCBF) data engine}\label{alg:safe-mppi}
\SetKwInOut{Input}{Input}\SetKwInOut{Output}{Output}
\Input{state $x_{k\mid k}$, horizon $N$, samples $M$, warm start $\bar u$, temperature $\lambda$, safety $\gamma$, model $f$}
\Output{safe control $u^*_{k\mid k}$}
build polytope $\mathcal P$ at $c=x_{k\mid k}$; set $(\mu,\Sigma)$ from $\mathcal P$ (Rmk.~1)\;
\For{$m=1$ \KwTo $M$}{
  \For{$i=0$ \KwTo $N-1$}{
    $u^{(m)}_{k+i}=\mu_{k+i}+\varepsilon^{(m)}_{k+i}$,\; $\varepsilon^{(m)}\!\sim\!\mathcal N(0,\Sigma)$; rollout $x^{(m)}_{k+i+1}$\;
    \If{$H_{\mathcal P}(x^{(m)}_{k+i+1})<(1-\gamma)H_{\mathcal P}(x^{(m)}_{k+i})$}{
      \textbf{reject}: $S^{(m)}\leftarrow\infty$\;
    }
  }
}
weight safe samples by \eqref{eq:mppi-update}; update $\bar u$; apply $u^*_{k\mid k}=\bar u_{k\mid k}$; shift\;
\end{algorithm}

Because the executed control is a convex combination of certified rollouts and each $\mathcal C_i$ is convex, the averaged plan stays safe:
\begin{proposition}\label{prop:1}
For a control-affine system, if all contributing rollouts satisfy $x^{(m)}_{k+1\mid k}\in\mathcal C_1$, then the MPPI-DCBF average $x^*_{k+1\mid k}=\sum_m\hat\omega_m x^{(m)}_{k+1\mid k}$ (with $\hat\omega_m\!\ge\!0$, $\sum_m\hat\omega_m\!=\!1$) also lies in $\mathcal C_1$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$x^{(m)}_{k+1\mid k}$ is affine in the control, so $x^*_{k+1\mid k}$ is a convex combination of the rollout states; a convex combination of points in the convex set $\mathcal C_1$ remains in $\mathcal C_1$.
\end{proof}
\noindent For an LTI system the same argument extends to the whole horizon ($x^*_{k+i\mid k}\in\mathcal C_i,\ \forall i$), so the averaged \emph{plan} --- and thus the warm start for the next step --- is safe, mirroring MPC-CBF \cite{zeng2021safety}. This requires only pointwise feasibility:
\begin{assumption}\label{ass:1}
For any $x_{k\mid k}\in\mathcal C_i$ there exists a control sequence keeping $x_{k+i\mid k}\in\mathcal C_i$ for all $i$.
\end{assumption}

\Remark[~2 (moving obstacles are harder)]{On static obstacles the polytope rejection is collision-free at all $\gamma$. With \emph{moving} pedestrians, a position-only polytope faces a genuine tension: a velocity-predictive face retreat anticipates closing agents but can \keyword{degenerate} the polytope (a face vanishes) in dense crowds, while no retreat stays feasible but myopic. This is the dominant residual-collision mode and motivates a velocity-aware (higher-order) barrier as future work.}

\section{Safety-Tunable Generative Policy}
We distill the engine into a real-time policy (Stage 3). A sequential encoder $g_\psi$ maps a short history of states/observations/controls to a context $c_t$ (resolving which side of an obstacle to pass), and a CFM head learns the velocity field conditioned on $(o_t,\gamma_t,c_t)$ by minimizing \eqref{eq:cfm}. At inference, $\gamma(k)$ is scheduled online and the ODE is integrated from noise to produce $\hat u_k$, applied receding-horizon.

\Remark[~3 (windowed generation)]{Rather than emitting a single control, the policy predicts a short \emph{control window} $U_{t:t+H_{\text{pred}}}$ and executes only its first entry ($H_{\text{exec}}{=}1$), re-planning each step. Windowing keeps the per-sample dimension small (raising the fraction of certifiable samples over one-shot whole-trajectory generation) while preserving temporal consistency, following action-chunked diffusion/flow policies \cite{chi2023diffusion, kurtz2025generative}.}

\section{Compact Polytope Certificate}\label{sec:cert}
The policy above inherits safety statistically. We now add a \emph{per-trajectory certificate}: a small optimization that, given a generated trajectory window $\{x_i\}_{i=0}^{H}$ and obstacles $\{(o_j,r_j)\}$, decides whether a safe polytope tube contains it.

\subsection{Maximum-margin polytope via an SOCP}
Given the window $\{q_t\}_{t=0}^{H}$ (with $c=q_0$) and obstacle disks $\{(o_i,r_i)\}$, we fit one face $a_i^\top(x-c)\le m_i$ per obstacle and ask that (i) every obstacle stays outside its face, and (ii) the whole trajectory stays inside the polytope \emph{scaled by the DCBF decay} $(1-\gamma)^t$. To keep the polytope compact we add $K$ \keyword{artificial boundary obstacles} evenly on a circle about $c$ (radius-$\rho_{\text{art}}$ disks at angles $\theta_\ell=2\pi\ell/K$; the ``$K$-gon'' of anchors in Fig.~\ref{fig:socp}). The certificate is the second-order cone program (SOCP)
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\max_{\{a_i,\,m_i\}}\;\; & \sum_i w_i\, m_i \\
\text{s.t.}\;\; & a_i^\top(q_t-c)\;\le\;(1-\gamma)^{t}\, m_i, \quad t=1,\dots,H,\\
& r_i\,\|a_i\|_2 \;\le\; a_i^\top(o_i-c)-m_i,\\
& \|a_i\|_2\le 1,\qquad m_i\ge m_{\min}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:socp}
\end{equation}
The first constraint keeps $q_t$ inside the $(1-\gamma)^t$-scaled polytope (the DCBF decay of \eqref{eq:dtcbf}); the second is a second-order cone that pushes obstacle $i$ (real or artificial) fully outside its tangent face; $\|a_i\|_2\le1$ fixes a unit normal so $m_i$ is a true margin. The window is \keyword{certified} iff \eqref{eq:socp} is feasible with all $m_i\ge m_{\min}>0$. With one positive-weight face per obstacle the blocks decouple, so each face admits an \emph{exact closed-form angular-interval} solution --- no numerical cone solver or $\theta$-sweep --- certifying a window in ${\sim}1$\,ms.

  \caption{Nominal radial faces vs.\ the variable-tangent SOCP \eqref{eq:socp} on a narrow gap. \textbf{Left:} fixed radial normals cannot fit between the two obstacles (\texttt{cert=False}). \textbf{Right:} optimizing each face normal (dashed: $K$ artificial boundary obstacles) yields a wider max-margin polytope that certifies the threading trajectory (\texttt{cert=True}, ${\sim}1$\,ms).}

Fig.~\ref{fig:socp} contrasts fixed radial faces (which fail to fit the gap) with the variable-tangent SOCP (which certifies it). The certificate is the bridge from statistical to provable safety:

\Remark[~4 (a certified sample proves safety)]{Unlike a soft cost, the certificate is \keyword{existential}: if the generative policy produces \emph{even one} trajectory whose window is feasible for \eqref{eq:socp}, that trajectory is provably collision-free, since $H_{\mathcal P}(x_i)\ge(1-\gamma)^iH_{\mathcal P}(x_0)>0$ places every $x_i$ strictly inside an obstacle-free polytope. Safety therefore reduces to \emph{sample existence}, which the next section actively pursues.}

\section{Safe Flow Expansion}
A conservative pretrained policy may cover only a subset of the certifiable behaviors (e.g.\ only passing below both obstacles). \keyword{Safe Flow Expansion} (Alg.~\ref{alg:sfe}) treats the polytope certificate as the black-box verifier $\tilde v$ of an active continued-pretraining loop that expands the policy's certified generable set, following the ActiveFlowExpansion template \cite{activeflowexpansion} with a single added line: \keyword{the verifier is the SOCP certificate \eqref{eq:socp}} and its input is a full closed-loop trajectory.

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{Safe Flow Expansion}\label{alg:sfe}
\SetKwInOut{Input}{Input}\SetKwInOut{Output}{Output}
\Input{pretrained policy $\mu_{\theta_0}$, SOCP verifier $\tilde v$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:socp}), rounds $T$, safety grid $\{\gamma\}$}
$\mathcal D_0\leftarrow$ verified broad proposals (seed diverse modes)\;
\For{$t=0$ \KwTo $T-1$}{
  self-generate closed-loop trajectories $x_{t+1}\sim p^{\theta_t}$ (exploration temperature)\;
  \textbf{query verifier} $y_{t+1}\leftarrow\tilde v(x_{t+1})$: certified iff collision-free $\wedge$ goal-reaching $\wedge$ Eq.~\eqref{eq:socp} feasible\;
  $\mathcal D_{t+1}\leftarrow\mathcal D_t\cup\{(x_{t+1},y_{t+1})\}$\;
  $\theta_{t+1}\leftarrow\textsc{UpdateFlow}(\theta_t,\mathcal D_{t+1})$ \tcp*[h]{$\triangleright$ mode-balanced replay}\;
  evaluate certified coverage; keep the best round\;
}
\Output{$\mu_{\theta^\star}$ at the peak certified-coverage round}
\end{algorithm}

\textsc{UpdateFlow} performs replay-based continued pretraining with the signed objective $g_t=\nabla\hat L_t^{+}(\theta_t)-\alpha_t\nabla\hat L_t^{-}(\theta_t)$ of \cite{activeflowexpansion}; we use $\alpha_t=0$, i.e.\ standard CFM \eqref{eq:cfm} on \emph{verifier-accepted} windows only. Two design choices matter:

\Remark[~5 (mode-balanced replay)]{Rare newly-discovered modes are easily forgotten if the accepted buffer is sampled uniformly. We tag each accepted window with its trajectory's behavioral mode and replay with inverse-frequency weights, so a freshly certified mode (e.g.\ a weave) is not washed out by the dominant one. Combined with keeping the peak-coverage round, this stabilizes discovery across rounds.}

\Remark[~6 (why a surrogate uncertainty is not the driver here)]{ActiveFlowExpansion optionally tilts self-generation toward high verifier-uncertainty regions using a linear kernel $k(x,x')=\langle\phi_s(x),\phi_s(x')\rangle$ over the velocity network's noised-flow features $\phi_s$. In our \emph{windowed} setting a design is a control window \emph{at a conditioning state}, so features of early vs.\ late windows are not directly comparable across a trajectory, and empirically the induced tilt is near-uniform. We therefore drive exploration with certified \emph{broad proposals} and sampling temperature, which is agnostic to the conditioning; the kernel machinery is retained only as a diagnostic.}

\section{Numerical Examples}
All data generation and training used a single NVIDIA H100 GPU.

\subsection{Double integrator: safe planning and $\gamma$-tunability}
On a 2D double integrator ($\Delta t=0.1$\,s) with $1$--$10$ random obstacles, MPPI-DCBF produces multi-modal collision-free data: lower $\gamma$ gives wide-berth detours, higher $\gamma$ tighter clearance (Fig.~\ref{fig:results_2d}). Against a penalty-only MPPI baseline on a single-obstacle benchmark (Table~\ref{tab:mppi_eval}), MPPI-DCBF attains $100\%$ success and far larger minimum clearance at comparable control cost and a few ms overhead. The CFM policy reproduces the distinct per-$\gamma$ modes and interpolates between them under an online $\gamma(k)$ schedule (Fig.~\ref{fig:flowmatching}).

\subsection{Certified expansion discovers new modes}
We stress the certificate and expansion on two multi-obstacle scenes: a \emph{narrow gap} (two vertically-stacked obstacles) and a \emph{slalom} (an upper and a lower obstacle, offset horizontally). The pretrained slalom policy is conservative --- it only passes \emph{below} both obstacles. Iterating Safe Flow Expansion (Alg.~\ref{alg:sfe}), the certified generable set grows \keyword{one behavioral mode at a time} (Fig.~\ref{fig:2x2}): first the \emph{weave} that threads the diagonal gap between the two obstacles, and --- only \emph{after} the policy has expanded into the weave --- the hardest \emph{over-the-top} mode that clears both obstacles from above. The bottom-right panel shows the certified coverage of the free space near the obstacles rising monotonically as modes are added; the top-right panel shows the noised-flow kernel $K=\langle\phi_s,\phi_s'\rangle$ \keyword{gaining block structure} (its effective sample size falls) exactly as the candidate windows separate into distinct modes --- the kernel is silent while the policy is uni-modal and becomes informative precisely when a new mode emerges. Fig.~\ref{fig:beforeafter} contrasts the pretrained single band with the final multi-modal certified set.

\section{Conclusion}
We presented a pipeline that makes a sampling-based planner safe and $\gamma$-tunable (MPPI-DCBF with a free-space polytope barrier), distills it into a real-time CFM policy, and --- crucially --- attaches a \emph{compact-polytope SOCP certificate} so that a single certified sample proves safety. Safe Flow Expansion then uses this certificate to expand the policy's certified generable set toward new safe modes, demonstrated on narrow-gap threading and slalom weaving. \keyword{Future work} targets the moving-crowd setting (Remark~2): a velocity-aware higher-order polytope barrier for the planner and certificate, and scaling Safe Flow Expansion to real pedestrian datasets (UCY/SDD), where the conservativeness--reach gap of the safe planner is exactly what certified expansion should close.
